For building an application, you should structure your research into clear data areas: breeds, farming systems, farmer workflows, seasonal challenges, health events, and market outputs. Here’s a fuller, app-focused understanding of cattle farming in South Africa.

1. Why this matters for an app
A cattle-farming app for South Africa could help farmers:

track herd records
monitor breeding and calving
plan feeding by season and region
record vaccinations and disease treatment
manage grazing and pasture rotation
track weights, sales, milk yield, and costs
support smallholders and commercial farmers differently
So your research should not only describe cattle farming, but also identify:

what information farmers need every day
what problems they face
what data changes over time
what decisions they must make

4. Farming systems in South Africa
A. Extensive farming
This is very common in South Africa, especially in beef farming.

How it works:

cattle graze over large areas of natural pasture
stocking rate depends on rainfall and veld condition
cattle may walk long distances for feed and water
farmers use fewer purchased inputs
Where common:

Limpopo
Free State
North West
Eastern Cape
parts of Mpumalanga and KwaZulu-Natal
Characteristics:

lower cost system
lower growth speed than feedlots
strong dependence on rainfall
indigenous breeds perform well
App features needed:

grazing camp records
rainfall logging
pasture condition scoring
water point monitoring
seasonal alerts for supplementation
B. Intensive farming / feedlots
This system is used mostly to finish cattle quickly for slaughter.

How it works:

animals are kept in smaller spaces
they are fed high-energy rations
growth is monitored closely
animals reach market weight faster
Characteristics:

higher costs
more controlled feeding
faster output
requires strong health and feed management
App features needed:

feed ration management
weight gain tracking
daily health checks
treatment records
market readiness indicators
C. Semi-intensive farming
This is a mix of grazing and supplementation.

How it works:

cattle graze but also receive feed supplements
used when pasture is not enough all year
common among many developing and medium-scale farms
App features needed:

both pasture and feed tracking
seasonal feed planning
simple cost recording

# 4Directions Farmer App — Cattle Module Developer Specification

## 1. Overview

Two production types are tracked individually per animal:

| Type | Breeds | Key records |
|---|---|---|
| Beef | Nguni, Bonsmara, Afrikaner, Drakensberger, Brahman, Angus, Hereford, Simmentaler, Charolais, Shorthorn | weight, ADG, FCR, calving, breeding, dipping, sales |
| Dairy | Holstein, Jersey, Ayrshire, Guernsey | milk yield, SCC, butterfat%, lactation, calving, breeding, dipping |

Sex vocabulary: `cow` `bull` `heifer` `steer` `calf_female` `calf_male`
FAMACHA is **not used** for cattle (goat/sheep only).
`DippingRecord` is cattle-specific.
`CalvingEvent` replaces `KiddingEvent`.

## 8. South African Compliance Rules

| Rule | Implementation |
|---|---|
| FMD zones | `fmdZone` field on animal; `FmdZoneIndicator` widget on detail Info tab; movement permit alert when crossing zones |
| NIIS/EID tagging | `niisEidNumber` field; shown on Info tab; add_cattle_screen includes EID field |
| Brucellosis testing | `brucellaTested` bool + `brucellaTestDate`; alert banner on detail if untested |
| Notifiable diseases | LSD, FMD, brucellosis, anthrax → `isNotifiable = true` on `CattleHealthEvent`; triggers `NotifiableDiseasePrompt` widget |
| Withdrawal periods | `withdrawalDaysMeat` + `withdrawalDaysMilk` on `CattleMedicationLog`; `WithdrawalCountdown` widget shown on detail Health tab while active |
| Dipping compliance | DAFF recommended dipping interval; `nextDueDate` computed on `DippingRecord`; overdue badge shown |
