Goat farming in South Africa is important in both the commercial and smallholder sectors.

Why goats matter in SA
They adapt well to dry and semi-arid environments
They can use browse, shrubs, and marginal land better than many other livestock
They provide:
meat
fiber
milk in limited systems
skins

income
cultural and ceremonial value
In communal and rural systems, goats often serve as:

4.2 Selection of breeding animals
Selecting breeding bucks
A breeding buck should have:

good health
strong legs and feet
sound reproductive organs
strong body condition
breed-appropriate structure
good performance background
no obvious defects
Because one buck serves many does, poor buck selection can damage the whole herd’s productivity.

Selecting breeding does
Choose does with:

good fertility
regular kidding history
strong mothering ability
good udder structure
ability to raise kids successfully
good health and adaptation
4.3 Breeding systems
Pure breeding
Used to maintain breed identity. Important in:

stud herds
registered breeders
breed improvement programs
Crossbreeding
Used to combine strengths of different breeds. Example:

improved meat buck x indigenous does
Possible benefits:

better growth
hybrid vigor
improved market goats
Risk:

poor planning can reduce uniformity and create inconsistent offspring
Upgrading
Repeated use of high-quality bucks on lower-performing females over generations. Useful for:

emerging farmers improving herd quality
4.4 Breeding control in communal systems
One of the major issues in South Africa is that many communal flocks use:

uncontrolled mating,
communal grazing,
unknown or inferior bucks,
limited record keeping.
This reduces genetic progress and can increase inbreeding risk. Research increasingly supports community-based breeding programs and better buck selection as practical solutions. [1][4]

4.5 Practical breeding management
Best practice includes:

using breeding seasons where possible,
selecting fertile bucks,
observing does for heat,
recording mating dates,
checking pregnancy,
preparing for kidding,
removing poor breeders from the herd.
Controlled mating improves:

kidding planning,
nutrition planning,
kid survival,
record accuracy.
4.6 Kidding management
Kidding management is essential because kid losses can seriously reduce profits.

Before kidding
provide clean, dry shelter
support late-pregnant does nutritionally
observe for labor signs
At kidding
supervise quietly
assist only if needed
avoid stress and rough handling
After kidding
ensure colostrum intake quickly
disinfect the navel if required in management protocols
make sure the doe accepts the kid
protect kids from cold, rain, starvation, and predators
Kid survival is one of the biggest practical success indicators in goat farming. [6][7]
